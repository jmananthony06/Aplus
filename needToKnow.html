Staying Away From Crowded Places and Close-Proximity Environments

Avoid close exposure to potentially sick or infected persons by practicing social distancing. Recommended precautions to take include washing your hands often and avoiding direct contact with your face. In addition, staying active, eating healthy and daily vitamin consumption will help improve your overall immune health.

Boosting Your Immunity

There's plenty of vitamin-enriched foods that you can add to your diet to help strengthen your immune system. Some of those items include, but are not limited to:

  • Citrus fruits
  • Red bell peppers
  • Broccoli
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Spinach
  • Yogurt
  • Almonds
  • Sunflower seeds
  • Tumeric
  • Green tea
  • Papaya
  • Kiwi
  • Poultry
  • Shellfish

No supplement will cure or prevent disease. With the COVID-19 pandemic, it's especially important to understand that no supplement, diet or other lifestyle modification other than social distancing and proper hygiene practices can protect you from COVID-19.

Who should not wear a face cloth or covering?

  • 1) Anyone who has breathing difficulties.
  • 2) Anyone who is unconscious, incapacitated, or otherwise unable to remove face covering without assistance or medical attention.
  • 3) Those caring for someone who may be sick with covid-19 at home or a non healthcare setting should also continue to practice daily preventative actions such as avoiding close contact with others as much as possible, cleaning hands often, and avoid touching eyes, nose or physical contact anywhere on your face.
